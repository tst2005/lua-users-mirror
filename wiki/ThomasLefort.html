What I like in Lua

  • "An extensible extension language" with a robust design.
  • Power with environments, metatables, closures and coroutines.
  • Simplicity with a clear syntax and a straightforward C API.
  • Fast, small and portable.

What I like less:

  • lack of standards, but that is also a source of flexibility.
  • lack of pattern-matching idioms, but again there is [MetaLua].

What I did, do and will do about Lua

  • advertise Lua, of course ! :)
  • help on IRC and mailing-list (according to my free time).

  • rewrite the [SWIG] module (work in progress as of Fall 2007) and then possibly [bootstrap] it.
  • debug Kepler's [RemDebug] (mostly done) and plug it into various editors; review RiciLake's [ldb].

  • debug the [SANO] collections framework.
  • review and contribute the so-called StandardLibraries.
  • beta-test [MetaLua].

  • more to come !
