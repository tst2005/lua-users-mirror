Two months ago, I was looking for small scripting language to replace a bunch of microperl scripts (Limited and ugly version of Perl). Now we have CLI, configuration wizards and init-scripts of our network devices rewritten in Lua. Asterisk AGI scripts will be rewritten soon. Two most important things about Lua are: 1) The code is much cleaner and flexible than microperl 2) Lua interpreter is significantly smaller than microperl (220 kb vs. 900 kb). This is very important for us because we're limited to ~5MB filesystem. For occasional programmer (like me) learning Lua takes ~2 weeks. // Dmitry Ivanov.

A few things listed below in decreasing order of importance... --DavidManura

  • On the page difference display, a unified diff likely will be much more helpful. I have difficulty quickly understanding changes made to pages (particularly when items get reorganized rather than edited) and often don't bother.
  • On the backlinks display, it could be helpful to include one sentence or paragraph of context for each backlink. This will show not just that "A links to B" but the more precise relationship between A and B. Furthermore, this will assist in checking that the citations for a given page are consistent.
  • I suggest adding a search box to the top of each page. Currently, one needs to scroll to the bottom of the page, click Find Page, and enter a search--and I use the search all the time. Prompting the user to select either "Title search" or "Full search" could be avoided--the search could automatically do both searches and display the title results first followed by the full search (though refinements options to the search could be displayed on the search results page if desired).

  • More types of syntax coloring could be added, mainly C/C++ and Lua interactive mode (e.g. snippets in the LuaTutorial).
  • Is there a way to link to a very recent mail list posting that is not yet indexed on (LuaList:...)? (This affected LuaNews especially.)
